Origin and Importance of Pranayama

Pranayama refers to a set of yogic breathing techniques that have their origins in ancient India. It plays a crucial role in balancing the mind and body, enhancing mental focus, increasing lung capacity, and promoting overall health.

Technique 1: Diaphragmatic Breathing

This technique emphasizes deep breathing by engaging the diaphragm for maximum air intake. Beneficial for stress reduction and improving cardiovascular health.

Technique 2: Box Breathing

Box breathing involves inhaling, holding the breath, exhaling, and holding again, each for a count of four. It helps in controlling anxiety and calming the mind.

Technique 3: Alternate Nostril Breathing

Alternate nostril breathing aims to bring balance to the mind and body by inhaling and exhaling through alternate nostrils, enhancing lung function and respiratory health.
